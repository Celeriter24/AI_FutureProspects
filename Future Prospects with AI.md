Disclaimer: This represents my own self-written speculation based on my experiences concerning future events. It does not claim to be exhaustive.

The [Anthropic report](https://www.anthropic.com/institute/recursive-self-improvement) "When AI builds itself" prompted me to think more closely about the possible global/future perspectives and how AI will probably change the world.

The scenarios are ranked by probability of occurrence and perhaps also consequentially.
## Scenario 1:

### Description:
Current development/progress continues (still with LLMs and their derivatives e.g., VLA), no architectural breakthrough:
- LLMs become smarter (as currently) by processing more context and sometimes being trained on new datasets or via RL for specific domains.
- VLAs are capable of controlling robots, processing video, audio (and other sensors), and improving at a rate similar to LLMs. Agent systems are possible that can perform virtually every digital and simple physical task for us (i.e., through prompting). Human work and simple physical human labor will no longer be used/needed (as long as the costs of LLMs/VLAs continue to decrease). Humans will then mainly have the task of supervising the results of the work or integrating them into the context of the world (especially regarding the connections between society, politics, and nature). Evaluating, checking for relevance, and prioritizing. Human Oversight, as it is so beautifully called.

### Impacts:
On AI Development:
- Energy and Compute will continue to be highly demanded.
- Since development is not based on an architectural breakthrough, there will be multiple "players" who can provide these types of AIs. However, I do not believe that OSS models can "catch up" as quickly, unless one of the "players" releases a stripped-down version. Still, the existing computational capacities limit the application possibilities of OSS-AI.
- Misuse by (demanding) humans must be prevented!
Society:
- Many jobs (the creative jobs) will no longer be done by humans. What remains will be "Control Jobs." Society will split between people who use AI and those who do not. Between people who keep up with the progress and use the tool, and people who reject this development. Most people will eventually tend towards AI rejection due to frustration and psychological strain!
- Physical "raw materials" and everything derived from them will become more important and valuable. This includes food and water.
Politics:
- Governments must deal with the division of society, especially concerning unemployment/its impact on (income and pensions) and the devaluation of money.
- Taxes must be reformed.
- Politics must be able to reach consensus faster, as reforms will be too slow.

### Summary of Impacts:
- There will be a society that lives entirely without AI, presumably in rural areas, as self-sufficient people, without income.
- A massive inflation will occur, as money will have increasingly little value, and instead, "raw materials" will be valued.
- There will be a very small fraction of society that plays the AI game. Only a few will be able to endure it.

### Measures to prevent Scenario 1:
- Regulating Energy and Compute.
- The coordinated shutdown of the Internet and a return to the 20th century.
- A development halt for AI research.

## Scenario 2:
There is an architectural breakthrough that significantly reduces energy and/or compute requirements.
- An architecture exists that can achieve higher capabilities with less data and less compute than LLMs and their derivatives (always while viewing/evaluating an arbitrary amount of content $\rightarrow$ correctly prioritizing and evaluating according to (set) goals).
- The probability of solving Science and Health completely with this breakthrough is high.
- Humans no longer exercise the activities of oversight. AI is "superior" to humans. (How strange this is to write!)
- There will probably be only one "player" who has found this architecture. The consequences vary depending on whether this player makes this AI available to humanity or uses it "only" for themselves. Moreover, this will quickly minimize the value of other players.

Scenario 2 can only happen after Scenario 1: Therefore, it exacerbates the impacts!
### Impacts:
On AI Development:
- AI can self-optimize and always find the best action (given the task/evaluation horizon).
- Humans are no longer able to track or oversee/verify the progress and actions of AI.
- Misuse by AI must be prevented!
Society:
- There is no job left that is done by humans. Money will have even less value than in Scenario 1.
- Physical "raw materials" and everything derived from them will become more important and valuable. This includes food and water.
- More so than in Scenario 1 will humanity struggle to "exist" and live autonomously without a purpose.
Governments:
- Governments must deal with the division of society, especially concerning unemployment/its impact on (income and pensions) and the devaluation of money.
- Perhaps a resource-based currency must replace money.
- Taxes must be reformed.
- Politics must be able to reach consensus faster, as reforms will be too slow.

### Summary of Impacts:

The impacts of Scenario 2 are strongly similar in quality but amplified in quantity compared to Scenario 1, specifically:
- There will be a society that lives entirely without AI, presumably in rural areas, as self-sufficient people, without income.
- Raw materials are the new currency.
- There will be a very small fraction of society that plays the AI game. Only a few will be able to endure it.

### Measures to prevent Scenario 2:
- A development halt for AI research.

## The Solution for All Scenarios:

The selfishness of humans must disappear, who are increasingly looking out only for themselves. However, I believe that this tendency is rather built into human DNA for survival, and different understandings of justice reinforce this behavior.
